Higher National Diploma in Accountancy




When was established HNDA?
           The Higher National Diploma in Accountancy (HNDA) was established in 1943 in the name of National Diploma in Accountancy with the aim of detecting, preventing frauds, errors, and malpractices in Ceylon Tea Estates and Ceylon Railway; and this was the first professional accounting course introduced back then in Sri Lanka (Ceylon). Later, this course was transformed into the Higher National Diploma in Accountancy (HNDA) in 1946.


Why should you apply?
Gradually, this course gained popularity and in 1963 part-time evening classes were also introduced to meet the market demand. Per Public Administration Circular No.46/90 of 1990, the HNDA qualification is considered as an equivalent( an alternate) to a Bachelors of Commerce degree (B. Com) offered by an accredited university, recognized by the University Grant Commission (UGC) of Sri Lanka. This course is mandated for auditing enterprises, excluding public sector organizations and quoted companies. The certified auditor's credentials were granted for the HNDA holders in 1968. Beginning 2001, the HNDA program is conducting courses throughout the Island by the Advanced Technological Institute (ATIs) which is administered by the Sri Lanka Institute of Advanced Technological Education (SLIATE). After completing the HNDA program, the HNDA holders can start their careers as registered auditors and can conduct audits of companies except for public sector organizations and quoted companies.


What will we be renderd for you?
The HNDA syllabus was lately revised after taking into consideration the Institute of Chartered Accountants of Sri Lanka (ICASL) and with the few other courses syllabus. The HNDA program can be completed on a full or part-time basis within four years. ATIs (formerly known as Technical Colleges) of SLIATE is conducting this program throughout the Island from its inception in 1965 with the mission in mind to produce excellent diploma holders of the higher education sector and for this purpose, the HNDA holders are taught to be skillful, competent, and also equipped with modern technology in the field of accounting and finance for contributing to the sustainable development of Sri Lanka.


What are qualifications you should have?
  1. Passes for all the subjects in one sitting at the G.C.E. (A/L) examination with credit pass for Economics and Accountancy.
  2. A credit pass in Mathematics at the G.C.E (O/L) is considered as an alternative for a credit pass in either Economics or Accountancy at the G.C.E (A/L) examination.
  3. An ordinary pass in the English Language at the G.C.E. (O/L) examination


How do you apply HNDA?
All duly filled applications should be directed to the relevant Advanced Technological Institute under a registered cover with a receipt obtained by crediting Rs 500 / - to the SLIATE account No 025-2-001-1-3397613 at peoples Bank, Park Street Branch ( Colombo), or any other branch of Peoples Bank in Sri Lanka.
